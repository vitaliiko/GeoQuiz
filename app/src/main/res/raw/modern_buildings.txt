The Mole Antonelliana is a major landmark building in Turin, Italy. It is named after the architect who built it, Alessandro Antonelli. A mole in Italian is a building of monumental proportions. Construction began in 1863, soon after Italian unification, and was completed in 1889, after the architect's death. Originally conceived of as a synagogue, it now houses the Museo Nazionale del Cinema, and is the tallest museum in the world.

The Tornado Tower, also called the QIPCO Tower, is a skyscraper in Doha, Qatar. The iconic building is 640 feet (195 m) tall with 52 floors. Construction was completed in 2008. The extravagant construction consists of a rhombic steel structure with floor-to-ceiling glazing and a column-free interior. The solar control glazing ipasol (special type) protects the interior from the torrid sun.
